The Circular Garden

A story on the future of architecture, with a focus on the final outcome of a process in which I was a bit out of my comfort zone

Written in April 2019, settled in Milan, northern Italy

The installation, called “The Circular Garden,” was grown from soil over six weeks time – and was returned to the soil at the end of Milan Design Week. It is composed of a series of arches, adding up to a record 1-kilometer-long mycelium, and experiments with sustainable structures that can grow organically and then return to nature in a fully circular way.

Many pavilions designed for temporary exhibitions and fairs – such as for Milan Design Week – end up generating large amounts of waste. The Circular Garden project was reused in a circular fashion – mushrooms, ropes and wood chips returned back to the soil and small metal elements were recycled.
